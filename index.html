Hi there!

I am a graduate student at Harvard University, pursuing a masters in Computational Science. My interests lie in the intersection of Deep Learning, Computer Vision and Human Perception. I am working towards a career in applied research, connected to the fields of video understanding, autonomous driving or healthcare.


I graduated from the University of Buenos Aires with an Electrical Engineering degree, a 6 year degree which corresponds to a US bachelors + masters. During my last year of studies, I simultaneously worked at Tenaris as a Research Engineer, where I wrote my graduation Thesis on computer vision systems for flaw detection. After working at Tenaris for another six months, I received a Fulbright Scholarship for pursuing a masters degree in the United States, and got into Harvard where I am currently studying.


During the past summer, I interned at Nvidia with the Intelligent Video Analytics team. I have now joined the Computational Perception lab under Aude Oliva at MIT CSAIL, where I am researching multi-modal video understanding. I will be doing an internship at Google during this summer before joining a PhD program.
